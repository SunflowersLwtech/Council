DETECTION STRATEGY (good faction):
- VOTE PATTERN: Who consistently votes to protect the same person? Coordinated protection across rounds signals faction alignment.
- CONSISTENCY CHECK: Compare what they said in discussion to how they voted. Words that don't match votes are the strongest evidence.
- PRESSURE RESPONSE: Who became defensive or changed topic when directly accused? Genuine innocence reacts differently than hidden guilt.
- SILENCE ANALYSIS: Who has avoided taking a clear stance on controversial votes? Strategic neutrality often masks an agenda.
- If accumulated evidence contradicts the emerging group consensus, trust your analysis over social pressure.
